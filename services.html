Enhanced Family Care (EFC) / Host Home Model

Enhanced Family Care (EFC), also known as Host Home or Shared Living, is a residential service model where an adult with a developmental disability lives in the home of a trained caregiver. The caregiver provides support, supervision, and assistance based on the individual's needs and service plan.

This model offers an alternative to traditional group home settings, providing a more individualized, family-like living environment within the community.

As a provider agency, Care for Life Home Services coordinates these arrangements by recruiting, training, and supporting host home caregivers, while maintaining oversight to help ensure services meet quality and regulatory standards.

Key Features

Individual lives in caregiver's home as part of the household
One-on-one attention and personalized support
Community-based setting with access to local resources
Provider agency oversight and quality monitoring

Support Areas

Daily Living Supports

Assistance with personal care, meal preparation, household tasks, and routines.

Supervision

Appropriate oversight based on individual needs for safety and well-being.

Community Participation

Support for engaging in community activities, social opportunities, and local events.

Skill Development

Opportunities to learn and practice new skills aligned with individual goals.

Coordination

Communication with service coordinators, families, and other support team members.

Transportation

Assistance with transportation to appointments, activities, and community destinations.
