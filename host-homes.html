Host Homes

Information for individuals interested in becoming a host home caregiver.

What Are Host Homes?

Host homes are private residences where trained caregivers provide residential supports for adults with developmental disabilities. The caregiver opens their home and provides daily support, supervision, and assistance based on each individual's needs and service plan.

This arrangement offers a more individualized, family-like living environment compared to traditional residential settings. Host home caregivers become an important part of the support network for the individuals they serve.

Host home caregivers work in partnership with Care for Life Home Services, which provides training, ongoing support, and oversight of the services provided.

Our Role as Provider Agency

Care for Life Home Services supports host home caregivers through:

Recruiting

Identifying and connecting with individuals who may be interested in becoming host home caregivers.

Screening

Background checks, reference verification, home assessments, and evaluation of caregiver readiness.

Training

Comprehensive initial training and ongoing professional development opportunities.

Monitoring

Regular oversight visits, documentation review, and quality assurance activities.

Ongoing Support

Access to agency staff for guidance, problem-solving, and assistance as needed.

Interested in Becoming a Host Home Caregiver?

If you're interested in learning more about becoming a host home caregiver in New Hampshire, we welcome your inquiry. The process begins with an initial conversation to discuss the role, responsibilities, and requirements.

General Requirements

  • Appropriate living space in your home
  • Ability to pass background check requirements
  • Completion of required training
  • Commitment to person-centered support practices
  • Valid driver's license and reliable transportation
