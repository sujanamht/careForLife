Training & Quality

Caregiver Training

All host home caregivers complete comprehensive training before providing services and participate in ongoing professional development. Training covers topics required by state regulations and best practices in the field.

Training Topics Include:

Rights & Dignity

Understanding & protecting individual rights

Confidentiality

Privacy practices and information protection

Emergency Preparedness

Planning for and responding to emergencies

Abuse Prevention & Reporting

Recognizing, preventing, and reporting abuse, neglect, and exploitation

Incident Reporting

Proper documentation and reporting of incidents

Documentation

Accurate record-keeping and service documentation

Person-Centered Practices

Supporting individual choice, preferences, and goals in service delivery

Professional Development

Ongoing Development

Training is not a one-time event. Caregivers participate in ongoing education and competency verification to maintain and enhance their skills.

  • Annual refresher training on core topics
  • Competency assessments to verify skill application
  • Individual-specific training based on support needs
  • Access to additional learning opportunities
Quality Assurance

Quality Assurance

We maintain quality assurance practices to help ensure services meet expectations and regulatory requirements.

Monitoring

Regular oversight visits to host homes, observation of service delivery, and check-ins with individuals and caregivers.

Documentation Review

Periodic review of service records, incident reports, and other documentation to identify areas for improvement.

Continuous Improvement

Using feedback, data, and identified trends to improve training, support, and service delivery practices.
